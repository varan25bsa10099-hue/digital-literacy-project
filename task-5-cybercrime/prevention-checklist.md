🛡️ Stay Safe Online: A Digital Hygiene Checklist

Target Audience: College Students & Young Professionals in India
General Digital Safety Tips
a. Strengthen Passwords: Use a unique, complex passphrase for your VIT Bhopal student portal and personal emails. Avoid using your date of birth or "123456."
b. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Activate 2FA on WhatsApp, Instagram, and LinkedIn to prevent account hijacking, even if your password is leaked.
c. Audit App Permissions: Regularly check which apps have access to your microphone, camera, and contacts. Delete "Clean-up" or "Battery Booster" apps that often contain adware.
d. Verify Official Domains: Always check for .gov.in or .nic.in when accessing government services like RTO, Aadhaar, or University portals.
e. Secure Public Wi-Fi: Avoid logging into banking apps or sensitive accounts while using free public Wi-Fi at airports or cafes; use a VPN or your mobile data instead.
f. Think Before You Click: Hover over links in emails or SMS to see the actual destination URL. If it looks like a string of random characters, do not click it.

Financial & UPI Safety (Mandatory)
a. The 'Receive' Rule: Remember that entering your UPI PIN is only for sending money, never for receiving it. If someone asks you to "Scan and enter PIN" to receive a refund, it is a scam.
b.  Verify UPI IDs: Before hitting 'Pay' for college fests or local vendors, double-check the verified name displayed on the UPI app to ensure it matches the recipient.

Emergency Reporting Channels 📞

If you or someone you know falls victim to a cybercrime:
a. Immediate Action: Call the National Cyber Crime Helpline at 1930.
b. Official Reporting: File a formal complaint at the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal: cybercrime.gov.in.
c. Bank Alert: Contact your bank’s "Report Fraud" division within the "Golden Hour" (first 2 hours) to increase the chances of reversing the transaction.
