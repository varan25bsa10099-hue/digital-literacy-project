Social Media Etiquette for B.Tech Students

Goal: To maintain a professional digital footprint that aligns with academic and career goals.
5 Do’s (Best Practices)

 a. Do update your privacy settings regularly on platforms like Instagram and Facebook to control who can view your personal information.
 b. Do use a professional and clear profile picture on career-oriented sites like LinkedIn and GitHub.
 c. Do verify the authenticity of a news story or academic link from a trusted source (like an official university handle) before sharing it.
 d. Do showcase your technical projects, certifications, and club involvements (e.g., Technomech club) to build a positive "digital trail."
 e. Do use two-factor authentication (2FA) on all social media accounts to prevent unauthorized access and identity theft.

 5 Don’ts (Risks to Avoid)
 
a. Don’t post offensive, discriminatory, or disrespectful comments, as these can be traced back to you by future recruiters or faculty.
b. Don’t share sensitive personal data such as your home address, phone number, or bank/UPI details in public posts or comments.
c. Don’t engage with "trolls" or participate in cyberbullying; instead, use the "block" and "report" features provided by the platform.
d. Don’t post photos or videos of your peers, professors, or campus events without obtaining explicit permission from everyone involved.
e. Don’t use "text-speak" or informal slang when communicating with professionals or faculty members on platforms like LinkedIn or via email.
